About Solar Energy

Understanding the most abundant energy source on Earth

What is Solar Energy?

Solar energy is radiant light and heat from the Sun that is harnessed using photovoltaic cells, solar thermal collectors, or concentrated solar power systems. It is an infinitely renewable resource — the Sun delivers more energy to Earth in one hour than humanity consumes in an entire year.

Unlike fossil fuels, solar power produces zero carbon emissions during operation, making it a cornerstone of India's clean energy transition.

How Solar Panels Work

Solar panels use the photovoltaic effect. When photons from sunlight strike silicon cells, they knock electrons loose, creating an electric current. This Direct Current (DC) is converted to Alternating Current (AC) by an inverter for home or grid use.

Multiple panels form an array, and multiple arrays form a solar farm. Excess electricity can be stored in batteries or fed back to the grid through net metering.

Solar Energy in India

India's incredible journey towards solar leadership

Current Status

India ranks 4th globally in solar power capacity. As of 2024, the country has surpassed 73 GW of installed solar capacity, with projects across all 28 states.

  • Solar Parks (Ultra Mega)
  • International Solar Alliance

  • Rewa, Madhya Pradesh – 750 MW

Advantages & Disadvantages

A balanced look at solar energy

Advantages

Zero Emissions

Produces no greenhouse gases during operation, fighting climate change.

Renewable & Infinite

Sunlight is free and will last for another 5 billion years.

Low Operating Cost

After installation, fuel cost is zero — only minimal maintenance required.

Energy Independence

Reduces reliance on costly coal and oil imports.

Job Creation

Solar sector has created over 1 million jobs in India.

Disadvantages

Weather Dependent

Output drops on cloudy or rainy days, requiring backup or storage.

High Initial Cost

Installation of panels and inverters requires significant upfront investment.

Large Space Needed

Utility-scale solar farms require vast land areas.

Storage Challenges

Battery storage is still expensive, limiting 24/7 solar supply.

Panel Disposal

Aging panels (after 25 yrs) create electronic waste challenges.

Applications of Solar Energy

From rooftops to fields — solar powers every sector of India

Residential

Rooftop solar panels power homes, reduce electricity bills by 70–90%, and earn credits through net metering. The PM Surya Ghar scheme offers subsidies to 10 million households.

Rooftop PV Net Metering Solar Water Heater

Industrial

Factories and warehouses use large solar arrays to cut energy costs. Companies like Tata, Reliance and ONGC have deployed captive solar plants across India.

Captive Plants Solar Parks Green Manufacturing

Agriculture

Solar-powered irrigation pumps replace diesel pumps, saving farmers thousands per year. PM-KUSUM targets installation of 3.5 million solar pumps by 2026.

Solar Pumps Cold Storage Agrivoltaics

Transport & EVs

Solar charging stations power electric vehicles across highways. India plans to solarize all railway stations and power Vande Bharat trains with renewable energy.

EV Charging Solar Railways Solar Boats

Future of Solar Energy in India

A bright roadmap to 2030 and beyond

2024

PM Surya Ghar

Free electricity for 10 million households via rooftop solar subsidies — one of the world's largest rooftop programmes.

2026

Green Hydrogen Mission

Solar-powered electrolysers will produce green hydrogen, enabling clean fuel for industries and heavy transport.

2027

Advanced Battery Storage

Grid-scale battery storage projects will solve intermittency, enabling 24/7 solar power delivery across India.

2030

500 GW Renewable Target

India aims for 500 GW renewable capacity — solar contributing 300 GW — cutting carbon intensity by 45% from 2005 levels.

2047

Viksit Bharat — Energy Independence

India's vision of becoming a developed nation by 2047, fully energy-independent powered by domestic renewable resources.

Perovskite Solar Cells

Next-gen cells with 30%+ efficiency, promising to revolutionise solar technology by 2030.

Space-Based Solar

ISRO is researching orbital solar panels that beam energy wirelessly to Earth — 24/7, weather-free.

Floating Solar (Floatovoltaics)

Solar panels on reservoirs reduce evaporation and generate electricity — India has 18,000+ water bodies suitable for this.

AI-Optimised Grids

Artificial intelligence will manage solar grid fluctuations in real time, maximising efficiency and stability.
